### **Key Highlights**

- **Explosive Market Growth**  
  The Flat Panel Satellite Antenna market is projected to soar, reaching **$6.7 billion by 2033**. This surge is driven by increasing demand for high-speed data transmission and innovative communication solutions. [Read more here](https://news.satnews.com/).

- **NASA's Dual Propulsion Experiment**  
  NASA has launched the **DUPLEX CubeSat**, featuring two new micropropulsion technologies. This mission aims to demonstrate innovative propulsion methods that could revolutionize satellite maneuverability and efficiency. [Learn more](https://www.nasa.gov/centers-and-facilities/ames/nasas-two-in-one-satellite-propulsion-demo-begins-in-space-test/).

- **Galileo's New Additions**  
  The European Union's **Galileo navigation system** received a major boost with the successful launch of **two new satellites**. This enhancement aims to improve precision in global positioning services. [Details available here](https://defence-industry-space.ec.europa.eu/galileo-gets-boost-two-new-satellites-successfully-launched-2025-12-17_en).

- **Innovative Satellite Architecture**  
  The US Space Force is set to launch four experimental satellites utilizing a new architecture called **DiskSat**. This design focuses on missions requiring high power and large apertures, marking a significant advancement in satellite engineering. [Explore more](https://www.ssc.spaceforce.mil/Newsroom/Article-Display/Article/4362043/space-force-prepares-for-dow-space-test-program-launch-of-four-experimental-sat).

- **In-Orbit Servicing**  
  Recent orbital rendezvous have paved the way for potential in-orbit satellite servicing, opening doors to maintenance and upgrades of satellites without returning them to Earth. This could significantly extend the lifespan of critical space infrastructure. [Read more](https://www.extremetech.com/aerospace/orbital-rendezvous-paves-the-way-for-in-orbit-satellite-servicing).
