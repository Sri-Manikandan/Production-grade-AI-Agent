1. **Runaway Supermassive Black Hole Detected**  
   The James Webb Space Telescope has made a groundbreaking discovery by confirming the existence of the first known "runaway" supermassive black hole, hurtling through the Cosmic Owl galaxies at an astonishing speed of 2.2 million mph. This finding challenges existing theories about black hole formation and dynamics. [Read more](https://www.space.com/news).

2. **Isaacman Takes the Helm at NASA**  
   Jared Isaacman has been confirmed as NASA's new administrator, a pivotal role as the agency aims for ambitious missions in the coming years. His leadership is expected to steer NASA towards exciting new frontiers in space exploration. [Discover more](https://www.cbsnews.com/space/).

3. **Interstellar Comet Approaches Earth**  
   An interstellar comet is making its closest approach to Earth, offering a rare opportunity to study a celestial visitor from beyond our solar system. As scientists prepare for this event, they are eager to gather data that may reveal insights about the origins of our solar system. [Learn more](https://www.cnn.com/science/space).

4. **European Ariane 6 Rocket Launches Navigation Satellites**  
   Europe's Ariane 6 rocket successfully deployed EU navigation satellites into orbit, marking a significant step in enhancing global navigation capabilities. This mission underscores the growing importance of satellite technology in our daily lives. [Find out more](https://phys.org/space-news/).

5. **NASA's Webb and Curiosity in TIME's Best Inventions Hall of Fame**  
   NASA's pioneering missions, including the Webb Space Telescope and the Curiosity rover, have been recognized in TIME's Best Inventions Hall of Fame, celebrating their contributions to our understanding of the universe. [Read the full story](https://www.nasa.gov).
