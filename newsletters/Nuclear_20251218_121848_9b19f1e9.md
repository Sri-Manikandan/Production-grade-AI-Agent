### 2. Trump's Nuclear Reactor Expansion
Recent executive orders from former President Trump have raised concerns regarding the safety of hastily approved nuclear reactor constructions across the United States. Experts are emphasizing the need for stringent safety measures as the nation considers expanding its nuclear capabilities. [Learn more from NPR.](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/17/nx-s1-5608371/trump-executive-order-new-nuclear-reactors-safety-concerns)

### 3. Significant Financial Investments
In a noteworthy move, Radiant has raised over $300 million to develop nuclear microreactors. This investment marks a substantial step towards innovative, smaller-scale nuclear solutions that could complement larger reactors in the energy mix. [Details available on Reuters.](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/nuclear/)

### 4. U.S. Department of Energy Funding
The U.S. Department of Energy announced $11 million in funding for five companies focusing on developing advanced transportation packages for nuclear materials. This initiative aims to enhance safety and efficiency in the nuclear supply chain. [Explore more from the Department of Energy.](https://www.energy.gov/ne/listings/ne-press-releases)

### 5. International Developments
Urenco USA has announced milestones in its efforts to reduce dependence on enriched uranium from Russia, highlighting global trends towards energy independence and security. Simultaneously, China has commenced the main construction phase of its Ningde-6 nuclear power plant, signaling robust growth in nuclear energy investments worldwide. [Further insights can be found on NucNet.](https://www.nucnet.org/)
